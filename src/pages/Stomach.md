## Overview

The Stomach in TCM is the **Rotting and Ripening Pot** — the Fu organ that receives food and drink, breaks them down, and hands the result to the [Small Intestine](SmallIntestine.md) for sorting. Paired with the [Spleen](Spleen.md) in the Earth phase, the Stomach is the receiving half and the Spleen is the transforming half — together they are the **central digestive engine** and the **post-natal source of Qi and Blood**. Most TCM digestive complaints involve some interaction of this pair.

## Primary function

The Stomach **receives, rots, and ripens** (_fu shou_, _fu shu_) food and drink. Its Qi naturally **descends** — sending the ripened result downward to the Small Intestine. When Stomach Qi rebels upward instead, the result is the canonical Stomach symptom picture: nausea, vomiting, hiccups, acid reflux, belching.
