## Overview

The Gallbladder in TCM is the **Decision-Maker** — the Fu organ paired with the [Liver](Liver.md) in the Wood phase. It stores and discharges bile (so far parallel with Western anatomy), but its larger role is symbolic: the Gallbladder governs **judgment and the courage to act on it**. A person who hesitates chronically, who second-guesses every decision, who is "timid" in the Chinese-medical sense, is read as having Gallbladder Qi deficiency. The Liver plans; the Gallbladder decides.

## Primary function

The Gallbladder **stores and discharges bile**, supporting the [Stomach](Stomach.md) and [Spleen](Spleen.md) in digestion (particularly of fats). On the psycho-emotional level, it governs **decisiveness and courage** — a function the Liver's planning cannot complete on its own. The Gallbladder is the only Fu that stores a "pure" substance (bile) rather than handling waste; some classics group it with the Extraordinary Fu for this reason.
