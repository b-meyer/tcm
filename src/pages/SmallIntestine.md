## Overview

The Small Intestine in TCM is the **Sorter** — the Fu organ that **separates the pure from the turbid** in what the [Stomach](Stomach.md) has ripened. Pure essence is sent up to the [Spleen](Spleen.md) for transformation into [Qi](Qi.md) and [Xue](Xue.md); turbid fluid is sent down to the [Bladder](Bladder.md) for excretion; turbid solid is sent on to the [Large Intestine](LargeIntestine.md). It is the Fu organ paired with the [Heart](Heart.md) in the Fire phase — a pairing that surprises Western intuition but reflects the shared theme of **discernment** (Heart discerns Shen; Small Intestine discerns substance).

## Primary function

The Small Intestine **receives, digests, and separates** what comes from the Stomach. Three streams emerge: pure → Spleen → Qi/Blood production; turbid liquid → Bladder; turbid solid → Large Intestine. A failure of this sorting underlies many TCM-framed diarrheas, edemas, and digestive-cognitive overlaps.
