## Overview

Herbal medicine is the **second branch** of the Five Branches of TCM Treatment and arguably its deepest body of knowledge. Plant, mineral, and animal substances — several hundred in routine use, several thousand in the full materia medica — are combined into **formulas** (_fang ji_) targeted at a diagnosed [Zang-Fu](ZangFu.md) pattern. The classical formularies (Shang Han Lun, Jin Gui Yao Lue, Wen Bing) catalog thousands of formulas, many still prescribed today.

## How it works

Each herb is profiled along four axes:

1. **Temperature** — Hot, Warm, Neutral, Cool, Cold. Warming herbs treat Cold patterns; cooling herbs treat Heat.
2. **Flavor** — Sour, Bitter, Sweet, Pungent, Salty (the [Five Flavors](WuXing.md), each linked to a phase and organ).
3. **Channel entered** — which [meridian(s)](Jingmai.md) the herb's action reaches.
4. **Direction of action** — ascending, descending, floating, sinking.

A formula combines herbs by role — _Jun_ (Sovereign), _Chen_ (Minister), _Zuo_ (Assistant), _Shi_ (Envoy) — to address the principal pattern while moderating side effects and guiding the action to the right channel. The pattern diagnosis ([BaGang.md](BaGang.md), [SiZhen.md](SiZhen.md)) is what tells the practitioner which formula to reach for.
