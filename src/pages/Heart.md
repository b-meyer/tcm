## Overview

The Heart in Traditional Chinese Medicine is not the muscular pump of Western physiology. Capitalized to distinguish it from its biological counterpart, the **Heart** is the **Emperor** of the body — the supreme organ-system whose stability dictates the stability of the whole. It governs the circulation of [Xue (Blood)](Xue.md) and houses the [Shen (Spirit/mind)](Shen.md). When the Emperor is settled, the kingdom is orderly; when the Emperor is agitated, the kingdom is chaos.

## Primary function

The Heart governs Blood and houses Shen. It propels Xue through the vessels and provides the stable resting place where Shen can dwell — meaning consciousness, sleep, memory, and emotional equilibrium all rest on Heart function. The classical phrase: _the Heart rules the Blood and stores the Shen_.

## Common patterns

Three pattern triad — the canonical Heart pathologies a practitioner screens for first.

- **Heart Fire** — Palpitations, mouth sores, vivid disturbing dreams, red tongue tip, rapid pulse; Heat agitates the Shen.
- **Heart Blood deficiency** — Difficulty falling asleep, poor memory, mild anxiety, pale thin tongue; often co-arises with Spleen deficiency (see [ZangFu.md](ZangFu.md) → Heart-Spleen Blood deficiency).
- **Heart Yin deficiency** — "Hollow" restlessness after midnight, low-grade night sweats, red tongue with little coating; a deeper depletion of the Heart's cooling aspect, and a primary driver of the Heart-Kidney axis pattern documented in [ZangFu.md](ZangFu.md).
