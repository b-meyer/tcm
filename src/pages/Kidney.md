## Overview

The Kidney in Traditional Chinese Medicine is the **Root of Life** — the constitutional reservoir that sources every other organ system. Capitalized to distinguish it from the bean-shaped filter of Western anatomy, the **Kidney** stores Pre-Natal [Jing (Essence)](Jing.md) inherited at conception and governs growth, reproduction, water metabolism, the bones, and the marrow that fills bone and brain. It is also the seat of **Zhi** (willpower) and the deep anchor of constitutional Yang.

_Note: "Shen" here is the Kidney organ; a different Chinese character with the same pinyin spelling refers to Spirit-Shen, covered in [Shen.md](Shen.md). See the [pinyin glossary](index.md#pinyin-disambiguation)._

## Primary function

The Kidney stores Jing (Essence), governs water metabolism, produces marrow, and **grasps Qi** by anchoring [Lung](Lung.md) inhalation — a grasping failure underlies many chronic asthmas. Kidney Yang is the foundational fire of the body; Kidney Yin is the foundational water. Every other organ's Yang and Yin ultimately leans on the Kidney's.

## Common patterns

Three pattern triad — the canonical Kidney pathologies a practitioner screens for first.

- **Kidney Yang deficiency** — Deep fatigue, cold lower back and knees, copious clear urine, dawn diarrhea, pale swollen tongue with white coat. Kidney Yang is the root of all bodily Yang, so [Spleen](Spleen.md) and [Heart](Heart.md) are often secondarily affected.
- **Kidney Yin deficiency** — Low back ache, afternoon flushing, night sweats, scanty dark urine, red tongue with little coating. Depleted Kidney Water fails to cool Heart Fire — a primary driver of the Heart-Kidney axis pattern documented in [ZangFu.md](ZangFu.md).
- **Kidney Essence depletion (Jing xu)** — Premature aging, early-onset sexual dysfunction, infertility, premature graying, cognitive decline. Jing replenishment is slow; tonifying herbs (He Shou Wu, Rou Cong Rong) and lifestyle conservation are central to treatment. See [Jing.md](Jing.md) for the substance itself.
