## Overview

The **San Jiao** (literally "Three Burners") is the most uniquely TCM of all organs — it has no Western anatomical counterpart and no physical structure at all. It is the **invisible thermal-regulation and fluid-coordination pathway** that runs through the three cavities of the torso: Upper Burner (chest, above the diaphragm), Middle Burner (epigastrium, above the navel), and Lower Burner (lower abdomen and pelvis). The San Jiao paired with the [Pericardium](Pericardium.md) is what completes the 6-organ symmetry that gives the meridian system its 12 channels ([Jingmai.md](Jingmai.md)).

## Primary function

The San Jiao **coordinates Qi and fluid metabolism across all three body cavities**. Each Burner has a characteristic image:

- **Upper Burner — "a mist."** The [Lung](Lung.md) and [Heart](Heart.md) disperse Qi and Blood as a fine vapor over the upper body.
- **Middle Burner — "a fermentation chamber."** The [Spleen](Spleen.md) and [Stomach](Stomach.md) ripen and rot food, extracting Gu Qi.
- **Lower Burner — "a drainage ditch."** The [Kidney](Kidney.md), [Bladder](Bladder.md), [Small Intestine](SmallIntestine.md), and [Large Intestine](LargeIntestine.md) separate clean from turbid and eliminate.
