## Overview

The Large Intestine in TCM is the **Drainage** organ — the final Fu in the digestive sequence that **receives turbid solid waste**, reabsorbs the last usable water, and **eliminates the residue**. It is paired with the [Lung](Lung.md) in the Metal phase; the pairing is more than anatomical — both organs share the theme of **letting go** (Lung releases the exhale and grief; Large Intestine releases waste). Many TCM treatments for grief and chronic constipation are framed through this pair.

## Primary function

The Large Intestine **receives the turbid solid** sent down by the [Small Intestine](SmallIntestine.md), reabsorbs water and fluids, and **transports and eliminates** the formed waste. When function is good, bowels move daily without strain; when function fails, the result is constipation (Heat/Dryness drying the stool, or Qi deficiency failing to propel) or diarrhea (Cold-Damp or Heat invasion).
