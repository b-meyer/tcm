## Overview

The Bladder in TCM is the **Reservoir** — the Fu organ that **receives, stores, and excretes** the turbid fluid that the [Kidney](Kidney.md) has separated from clean fluid. Paired with the Kidney in the Water phase, the Bladder's function is wholly dependent on Kidney Yang for the warmth that drives both storage (not leaking) and timely release. The Bladder channel is also the **longest meridian in the body**, running from the inner corner of the eye over the head and down the entire back to the little toe — which makes it the workhorse channel for back pain, sciatica, and many systemic complaints.

## Primary function

The Bladder **stores and excretes the urine** produced by the Kidney's water-metabolism work. The Qi transformation that turns metabolic byproduct into excreted urine requires Kidney Yang; a failure of that warming function shows up as frequent clear urination, incontinence, or — in the opposite direction — retention.
